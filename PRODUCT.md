# Product

## Register

product

## Users
Photographers (Fujifilm shooters and anyone editing photos) — primarily Tristan / Studio Réverbère. Context: mobile-first, often on a phone, browsing film looks and applying one to their own photo. The job: find a film-simulation recipe, see it on their image, tweak it, and export.

## Product Purpose
**Fudge** turns 1058 community Fujifilm film-simulation recipes into applicable presets with classic photo-app sliders, plus a real per-pixel rendering engine that reinterprets each recipe (white balance, per-simulation colour science, tone, grain) so the user can preview and bake the look onto their own photo. Success = the user quickly finds a look they love, sees it faithfully on their photo, and exports it.

## Brand Personality
Fudge — utilitarian, confident, photographic. Three words: **precise, analog, unfussy.** Monochrome chrome (black / white / grey) — neutral, gets out of the way. The interface is a darkroom tool: it gets out of the way of the image.

## Anti-references
- Generic AI/SaaS look (cards-everywhere, gradient text, tiny tracked eyebrows, glassmorphism).
- Official Fujifilm / camera-menu skeuomorphism.
- Over-decorated "creative tool" chrome that competes with the photo.

## Design Principles
- **The photo is the hero.** Chrome is monochrome (black/white/grey); all colour lives in the photo previews.
- **Earned familiarity.** Standard photo-app affordances (sliders, segmented controls, preset grid) — no invented controls.
- **Faithful first.** The render must reflect the recipe (esp. white balance); pretty-but-wrong is failure.
- **One consistent vocabulary.** Same button, chip, slider, segment shapes everywhere.

## Accessibility & Inclusion
Target WCAG AA for text/controls. Dark monochrome theme; ensure white-on-black and text-on-white (black ink) contrast pass. Respect `prefers-reduced-motion`. Touch targets ≥44px on mobile.
